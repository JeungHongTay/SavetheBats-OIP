Ideation & Brainstorming

Initial Brainstorming Sessions

Our team conducted several collaborative brainstorming sessions to explore different approaches to bat conservation and light pollution monitoring. We used techniques like mind mapping, "How Might We" questions, and crazy 8s to generate diverse ideas.

Key Questions We Explored:

  • How might we make bat conservation data accessible to the public?
  • How might we encourage community participation in wildlife protection?
  • How might we provide actionable insights to city planners?
  • How might we visualize the relationship between lighting and bat behavior?

Ideas Generated:

  • Real-time bat activity dashboard
  • Adopt-a-bat community program
  • Light pollution heat maps
  • Mobile alert system for bat researchers
  • Interactive education platform
  • AR park navigation with bat information

High-Fidelity Design in Figma

Visual Design Evolution

Moving from paper sketches to digital prototypes, we refined our visual design language, focusing on accessibility, data clarity, and emotional connection to bat conservation.

Design Principles:

  • Clarity: Clear data visualization that tells a story
  • Accessibility: High contrast, readable fonts, mobile-friendly
  • Emotional Connection: Beautiful imagery that connects users to nature
  • Trust: Professional appearance that conveys scientific credibility

Key Design Decisions:

Color Palette: Dark blues and greens reflecting nighttime nature
Typography: Inter font family for excellent readability
Icons: FontAwesome for consistency and recognition
Charts: Interactive visualizations using Chart.js

User Testing & Feedback

Testing Methods

  • Usability Testing: 5 participants testing key user flows
  • Stakeholder Reviews: Feedback sessions with council representatives
  • Expert Evaluation: Heuristic evaluation by UX professionals
  • Accessibility Testing: Testing with screen readers and color blindness simulation

Key Insights & Changes

Navigation Confusion

Problem: Users unclear about difference between "Analytics" and "Dashboard"

Solution: Simplified navigation to single "Dashboard" with tabbed sections

Data Overwhelm

Problem: Too much data presented at once confused casual users

Solution: Progressive disclosure with summary cards and detailed views

Mobile Charts

Problem: Complex charts difficult to read on mobile devices

Solution: Simplified mobile charts with option to view full data
