Problem & Solution

Understanding the impact of light pollution on Glasgow's bat populations and our comprehensive analytics dashboard approach

The Problem: Light Pollution and Bat Conservation

Glasgow's urban environment, particularly during events like GlasGLOW, creates significant challenges for local bat populations. Artificial Light at Night (ALAN) disrupts natural bat behaviors, affecting their feeding patterns, navigation, and habitat use.

Key Challenges Identified:

  • Disrupted Flight Patterns: Artificial lighting interferes with bat echolocation and natural navigation
  • Reduced Feeding Efficiency: Light pollution affects insect populations that bats depend on
  • Habitat Fragmentation: Bright lights create barriers between roosting and feeding areas
  • Lack of Real-time Data: No comprehensive monitoring system for bat activity and light pollution correlation
  • Limited Species Tracking: Difficulty identifying which species are most affected by different light types
  • Public Awareness Gap: Citizens unaware of their lighting choices' impact on local wildlife
  • Policy Gaps: Insufficient data-driven recommendations for lighting policies

Context & Stakeholders

Glasgow City Council has identified wildlife conservation as a priority, particularly in balancing urban development with ecological preservation. The GlasGLOW winter festival, while culturally significant, presents a case study in managing temporary light installations alongside permanent urban lighting.

Our Solution: Comprehensive Analytics Dashboard

Bat Activity Monitoring

Real-time bat count, species detection, interactive species cards, and monthly activity trend analysis

Light Pollution Tracking

Live light intensity readings, sensor network mapping, spectral composition analysis, and historical trends

Impact Analysis

Correlation charts, event impact comparison, species sensitivity analysis, and policy recommendations

Why This Approach?

Why an Analytics Dashboard?

A comprehensive analytics dashboard makes complex ecological data accessible to stakeholders while providing real-time insights for immediate conservation actions. The visual interface enables both scientific analysis and public engagement through interactive features.

Why Sensor Networks?

Continuous monitoring provides accurate, objective data rather than relying on periodic manual surveys. IoT sensors offer cost-effective, scalable monitoring across multiple park locations.

Why a Web Application?

Cross-platform accessibility ensures council officials, researchers, and citizens can access data from any device. Cloud-based architecture enables real-time collaboration and data sharing.

Alternative Approaches Considered

Manual Bat Surveys

Considered: Traditional periodic bat counts by researchers

Rejected because: Limited frequency, high cost, subjective data, doesn't provide real-time insights

Static Lighting Guidelines

Considered: Fixed lighting regulations without monitoring

Rejected because: No feedback loop, doesn't account for seasonal variations or specific local conditions

Mobile App Only

Considered: Smartphone app for citizen reporting

Rejected because: Relies on subjective reports, limited scientific accuracy, poor data consistency

Our Hybrid Approach

Selected: Sensor network + web dashboard + community features

Advantages: Objective data, real-time monitoring, stakeholder engagement, scalable solution
