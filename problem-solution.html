Problem & Solution

Understanding the impact of light pollution on Glasgow's bat populations and our comprehensive analytics dashboard approach

The Problem: Light Pollution and Bat Conservation

Glasgow's urban environment, particularly during events like GlasGLOW, creates significant challenges for local bat populations. Artificial Light at Night (ALAN) disrupts natural bat behaviors, affecting their feeding patterns, navigation, and habitat use.

Key Challenges Identified:

Lack of Consistent Data Collection

Past data collection was done only once, not annually or continuously. No year-round biodiversity monitoring exists, making accurate trend analysis impossible.

Artificial Light at Night (ALAN) Impact

Annual GlasGLOW events occur during sensitive times for bats and insects, disrupting feeding, roosting, hibernation, and ecosystem health through skyglow.

No Real-Time Monitoring Infrastructure

No sensors monitor light intensity or bat activity. No live dashboard exists, and all data capture is manual and unsustainable.

Weak Governance and Accountability

Council prioritizes revenue over biodiversity. Impact assessments are infrequent (every 5 years) and often paid by event organizers, creating conflicts of interest.

Lack of Resources and Manpower

No tech infrastructure or dedicated team for sensor installation, maintenance, or data review. Volunteers lack technical expertise for system upkeep.

Need for Zone-Based Monitoring

Light pollution varies across the Botanics - some areas are darker near River Kelvin while others face street lighting exposure, requiring multi-point sensor deployment.

Context & Stakeholders

Glasgow City Council has identified wildlife conservation as a priority, particularly in balancing urban development with ecological preservation. The GlasGLOW winter festival, while culturally significant, presents a case study in managing temporary light installations alongside permanent urban lighting.

Why This Approach?

Why an Analytics Dashboard?

A comprehensive analytics dashboard makes complex ecological data accessible to stakeholders while providing real-time insights for immediate conservation actions. The visual interface enables both scientific analysis and public engagement through interactive features.

✓ Real-time Insights ✓ Visual Interface ✓ Public Engagement

Why Sensor Networks?

Continuous monitoring provides accurate, objective data rather than relying on periodic manual surveys. IoT sensors offer cost-effective, scalable monitoring across multiple park locations.

✓ Continuous Monitoring ✓ Objective Data ✓ Cost-effective

Why a Web Application?

Cross-platform accessibility ensures council officials, researchers, and citizens can access data from any device. Cloud-based architecture enables real-time collaboration and data sharing.

✓ Cross-platform ✓ Cloud-based ✓ Real-time Collaboration

Why This Matters

Without year-round data collection, there's no way to track biodiversity loss or measure the damage of large-scale events like GlasGLOW. This risks not only wildlife, but also undermines Scotland's legal and ethical obligations to protect its environment.
