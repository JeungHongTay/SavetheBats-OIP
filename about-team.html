Meet Our Team

The passionate individuals behind EcoNoctis Glasgow - combining technology and conservation expertise

Our Multidisciplinary Approach

EcoNoctis Glasgow was developed by a diverse team of four students from the Singapore Institute of Technology, each bringing unique expertise to tackle the complex challenge of urban wildlife conservation. Our interdisciplinary approach combined technical skills with environmental science knowledge and user-centered design principles.

Working together over eight intensive weeks, we learned that successful conservation technology requires not just technical innovation, but also deep understanding of stakeholder needs, environmental science, and community engagement strategies.

How We Worked Together

Collaboration Methodology

Our team adopted an agile approach with weekly sprints, daily check-ins, and collaborative design sessions. We used a combination of in-person meetings and digital collaboration tools to maintain momentum across different schedules and time zones.

Communication Strategy

We established clear communication protocols using Slack for daily updates, Zoom for meetings, and Figma for collaborative design work. Regular retrospectives helped us identify and address collaboration challenges early.

Decision-Making Process

Major project decisions were made collectively through structured discussions and evidence-based evaluation. We used techniques like dot voting and impact-effort matrices to prioritize features and resolve disagreements constructively.

What We Learned About Teamwork

Communication is Everything

Clear, frequent communication prevented misunderstandings and kept everyone aligned on goals. Regular check-ins and transparent sharing of challenges led to better outcomes than trying to solve problems in isolation.

Diverse Perspectives Strengthen Solutions

Having team members from different academic backgrounds led to more innovative and comprehensive solutions. Environmental science insights improved technical designs, while UX expertise made complex data more accessible.

Iteration Requires Trust

Willingness to receive feedback and iterate on ideas required building trust within the team. Creating a safe environment for constructive criticism led to dramatically better outcomes than our initial individual efforts.

Balancing Leadership and Collaboration

While Sarah served as project lead, decision-making remained collaborative. Learning when to lead and when to follow based on expertise rather than hierarchy improved both our process and results.

Our Project's Legacy

What We Accomplished

  • Created a functional prototype addressing a real environmental challenge
  • Engaged with genuine stakeholders and incorporated their feedback
  • Developed a scalable solution that could be adapted to other cities
  • Built strong collaborative relationships and professional skills
  • Demonstrated the potential for technology to support conservation efforts

Potential Real-World Impact

While developed as an academic project, EcoNoctis Glasgow has generated genuine interest from Glasgow City Council for potential pilot implementation. Our approach demonstrates how student projects can address real-world challenges and contribute meaningful solutions to complex environmental problems.

Skills We'll Carry Forward

Beyond technical abilities, this project developed crucial professional skills in project management, stakeholder communication, iterative design, and collaborative problem-solving. These competencies will serve us well in future academic and professional endeavors.
