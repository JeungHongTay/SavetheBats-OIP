Meet Our Team

The team behind EcoNoc Glasgow's comprehensive analytics dashboard for bat conservation

Our Approach

EcoNoc Glasgow was developed by a diverse team of four students from the Singapore Institute of Technology, each bringing unique expertise to tackle the complex challenge of urban wildlife conservation. Our interdisciplinary approach combined technical skills with environmental science knowledge and user-centered design principles.

Working together over these three intensive weeks. Our team came to understand the difficulty in tackling this tough project where consistent data collection was required. We began to understand the depth of the issue. Where the data needed to be not only consistently collected, it had to be low cost, non-intrusive to the parks greenery while also easily maintainble by park staff.

We hope that EcoNoc showcases how Consistent Data collection and Visually Appealing Dashboard can be achieved with low costs and low maintenance solution.

How We Worked Together

Collaboration Methodology

Our team adopted an agile approach with weekly sprints, daily check-ins, and collaborative design sessions. We used a combination of in-person meetings and leveraged digital collaboration tools like figma and canva

Communication Strategy

We established clear communication protocols using Telegram for daily updates, Zoom for meetings, and Figma for collaborative design work. Regular retrospectives helped us identify and address collaboration challenges.

Decision-Making Process

Major project decisions were made collectively through structured discussions and evidence-based evaluation. We made sure each opinion was heard and discussed before coming to a final decision. Making use of conflict resolution techniques like constructive feedback and compromise to ensure any disagreements were resolved amicably.
